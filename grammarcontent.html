Grammar Questions

1. Choose the correct form: She ___ to school every day.

Correct Answer: b. Goes
Explanation: "Goes" is the correct form for the subject "She."

2. They ___ playing football when it started to rain.

Correct Answer: a. Were
Explanation: "Were" is the correct form for the plural subject "They."

3. What is the correct form? "I wish I ___ the answer."

Correct Answer: b. Knew
Explanation: "Knew" is the correct past tense form for the subjunctive mood.

4. Choose the correct sentence:

Correct Answer: b. He goes to school.
Explanation: "Goes" is the correct present tense form.

5. What is the past tense of 'eat'?

Correct Answer: b. Ate
Explanation: "Ate" is the correct past tense form of "eat."

6. They ___ to the party last night.

Correct Answer: c. Went
Explanation: "Went" is the correct past tense form of "go."

7. I have ___ the book you lent me.

Correct Answer: a. Read
Explanation: "Read" is the correct past participle form of "read."

8. Choose the correct word: "She is better ___ swimming than I am."

Correct Answer: a. At
Explanation: "Better at" is the correct preposition to use.

9. What is the plural form of 'child'?

Correct Answer: b. Children
Explanation: "Children" is the correct plural form of "child."

10. Choose the correct tense: "She ___ to the store tomorrow."

Correct Answer: d. Will go
Explanation: "Will go" is the correct future tense form.

11. Identify the correct sentence:

Correct Answer: c. He doesn't like pizza.
Explanation: "Doesn't" is the correct negative form.

12. Fill in the blank: "If I ___ you, I would apologize."

Correct Answer: b. Were
Explanation: "Were" is used in the subjunctive mood.

13. What is the comparative form of 'good'?

Correct Answer: c. Better
Explanation: "Better" is the correct comparative form of "good."

14. Identify the mistake in this sentence: "She don't have any money."

Correct Answer: a. Don't
Explanation: "Doesn't" should be used instead of "don't."

15. Choose the correct preposition: "He is good ___ math."

Correct Answer: b. At
Explanation: "Good at" is the correct preposition to use.
