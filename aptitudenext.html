Aptitude Topics

1. Numbers

Formulas:

  • Divisibility by 2: A number is divisible by 2 if its last digit is even.
  • LCM of two numbers: LCM = (a * b) / GCD(a, b)
  • Prime factorization: Expressing a number as a product of its prime factors.
  • Factorial: n! = n × (n-1) × (n-2) × ... × 1
  • Sum of first n natural numbers: S = n(n + 1)/2

Example Questions:

1. What is the LCM of 12 and 15?

Answer: 60

2. Find the sum of the first 10 natural numbers.

Answer: 55

3. Is 29 a prime number?

Answer: Yes

4. What is 5 factorial (5!)?

Answer: 120

5. Find the GCD of 48 and 60.

Answer: 12

2. Percentage

Formulas:

  • Percentage formula: (Value / Total Value) × 100%
  • Percentage increase = [(New Value - Old Value) / Old Value] × 100%
  • Percentage decrease = [(Old Value - New Value) / Old Value] × 100%
  • Profit Percentage = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100%
  • Loss Percentage = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100%

Example Questions:

1. What is 20% of 150?

Answer: 30

2. If the price increases from 100 to 120, what is the percentage increase?

Answer: 20%

3. Find 15% profit on a cost price of 200.

Answer: 30

4. A product was sold at a loss of 10%, if the cost price was 500, what was the loss?

Answer: 50

5. Find the percentage decrease if a value drops from 300 to 240.

Answer: 20%

3. Profit and Loss

Formulas:

  • Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price
  • Loss = Cost Price - Selling Price
  • Profit Percentage = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100%
  • Loss Percentage = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100%
  • Discount = Marked Price - Selling Price

Example Questions:

1. If the cost price is 100 and selling price is 120, what is the profit?

Answer: 20

2. If a shopkeeper sold a product at a 10% loss, what is the selling price of a 200 item?

Answer: 180

3. Find the profit percentage if a product was bought for 250 and sold for 300.

Answer: 20%

4. If a product with a marked price of 500 was sold at a 10% discount, what is the selling price?

Answer: 450

5. Find the loss percentage if a product was bought for 600 and sold for 480.

Answer: 20%

4. Average

Formulas:

  • Average = (Sum of all values) / (Number of values)
  • If all numbers are increased by the same value, the average increases by the same value.
  • If all numbers are multiplied by the same value, the average is also multiplied by that value.
  • Average of first n natural numbers = (n + 1) / 2
  • Average of consecutive numbers = (First Number + Last Number) / 2

Example Questions:

1. Find the average of 10, 20, 30, 40, and 50.

Answer: 30

2. The average of 5 numbers is 20. If one of the numbers is 10, find the sum of the other four numbers.

Answer: 90

3. The average weight of 8 men is 75 kg. One man weighing 80 kg leaves and a new man weighing 70 kg joins. What is the new average weight?

Answer: 74.375 kg

4. Find the average of the first 50 natural numbers.

Answer: 25.5

5. The average of 10, 20, and 30 is added to the average of 40, 50, and 60. What is the result?

Answer: 70

5. Ratio and Proportion

Formulas:

  • If a : b = c : d, then a/b = c/d.
  • Ratio of two quantities a and b = a : b.
  • Proportion: If a/b = c/d, then a : b :: c : d.
  • Sum of the terms in ratio a : b = a + b.
  • If a/b = c/d = e/f = ..., then (a + c + e + ...) / (b + d + f + ...) = a/b.

Example Questions:

1. If the ratio of ages of two people is 3:5 and the sum of their ages is 40, find their ages.

Answer: Ages are 15 and 25.

2. Divide $600 in the ratio 2:3 between two people.

Answer: $240 and $360.

3. If 2 : 3 :: 4 : x, find the value of x.

Answer: x = 6.

4. A recipe requires ingredients in the ratio 4 : 5. If 20g of the first ingredient is used, how much of the second is needed?

Answer: 25g of the second ingredient.

5. Find the third proportional to 4 and 8.

Answer: 16.

6. Mixture and Alligation

Formulas:

  • Mean Price = (C1 × Q1 + C2 × Q2) / (Q1 + Q2).
  • Alligation Rule: (C2 - Mean) : (Mean - C1).
  • Final Quantity Ratio in a Mixture = C1 : C2.
  • For repeated replacement, Quantity left = Initial Quantity × (1 - Replaced Fraction)^n.
  • Final mixture price = (Price1 × Qty1 + Price2 × Qty2) / Total Qty.

Example Questions:

1. Mix two types of rice at Rs. 20/kg and Rs. 30/kg to get a mixture at Rs. 25/kg. Find the ratio of quantities.

Answer: 1 : 1.

2. A 20L mixture has a 3:1 milk to water ratio. How much water to add to make it 1:1?

Answer: Add 10L of water.

3. How much water should be added to 60L of a 3% salt solution to make it 1% salt?

Answer: Add 120L of water.

4. In a 60L mixture of milk and water in 7:5, how much milk is there?

Answer: 35L of milk.

5. Find the quantity ratio to mix liquids costing Rs. 30 and Rs. 50 per liter to get a mixture at Rs. 40.

Answer: 1 : 1.

7. Time and Work

Formulas:

  • If A can do work in X days, then A's 1 day's work = 1/X.
  • If A is X times as efficient as B, ratio of work done by A : B = X : 1.
  • Work = Rate × Time.
  • Combined work rate of A and B = A's rate + B's rate.
  • If A, B, and C can do work alone in X, Y, Z days, together they can do work in 1/(1/X + 1/Y + 1/Z) days.

Example Questions:

1. A can finish work in 5 days, B in 10 days. How many days if they work together?

Answer: 3.33 days.

2. If A completes work in 6 days and B in 8, what fraction of work is left after 2 days of working together?

Answer: 5/12 work left.

3. C is twice as efficient as D. Together they can do a job in 12 days. How long will it take D alone?

Answer: 36 days.

4. A, B, and C can complete work in 10, 20, and 30 days, respectively. How many days if all work together?

Answer: 5.45 days.

5. If A is 50% more efficient than B and together they complete work in 18 days, how long would B take alone?

Answer: 45 days.
